Get help now

  • 211 Find local help for shelter, food, rent, utilities, health care, and more. Dial 211 in many areas.
  • USICH: How to Get Help Federal guidance on how to find local help (211 and local Continuum of Care).
  • USA.gov: Emergency housing Federal starting point for finding emergency housing help.
  • 988 Suicide and Crisis Lifeline Call or text 988 for crisis support (24/7).
  • National Domestic Violence Hotline Support, safety planning, and local referrals (call, chat, text options).

Housing and homelessness help

  • HUD: Continuum of Care (CoC) Local systems that connect people to shelter, rapid rehousing, and supportive housing.
  • HUD: Find Shelter Search for shelter and services by location.
  • HUD: Emergency Housing Vouchers (EHV) Voucher program for people experiencing homelessness or at risk (access is typically through local partners).
  • USICH (US Interagency Council on Homelessness) Federal agency that coordinates the federal response to homelessness and publishes guidance and resources.
  • USICH: Federal guidance and resources Case studies, fact sheets, and webinar recordings for communities and providers.

Food and basic needs

  • USDA: SNAP Food benefits (apply through your state).
    State offices: SNAP state directory
  • USDA: WIC Nutrition support for pregnant people, infants, and children (apply locally).
  • Feeding America: Find a food bank Find local food pantries and food banks.
  • HHS: TANF Cash assistance and support services for eligible families (run by states).

Health, mental health, recovery

  • Medicaid.gov Health coverage information and how to apply (state-based).
  • HealthCare.gov Marketplace coverage options and enrollment.
  • FindTreatment.gov Locator for mental health and substance use treatment.
  • SAMHSA National Helpline Confidential help and treatment referrals.

Jobs, training, certifications

  • American Job Centers (CareerOneStop) Job search, resumes, career coaching, and training referrals (including WIOA through local workforce boards).
  • DOL: WIOA overview Federal workforce training framework (services delivered locally).
  • Apprenticeship.gov Find paid apprenticeships and training programs.
  • Job Corps No-cost education and job training for eligible ages 16 to 24 (often includes housing).
  • Goodwill: Jobs and training Job help and training programs (availability varies by local Goodwill).
  • Coursera Online courses; some may be low-cost or available via financial aid (check each course).
  • edX Online courses; some are free to audit, certificates often paid.
  • LinkedIn Learning Many public libraries provide free access. Ask your local library.

Self sufficient living and simple independence ideas

  • Find a community garden (ACGA map) Use the map to find a community garden near you, or to learn how gardens are organized.
  • Community garden resources (ACGA) Guides and links for starting and managing a community garden.
  • USDA Urban Agriculture Programs Federal support and education for growing food in cities and small spaces.
  • Penn State Extension: Microgreens guide Microgreens basics and how to grow them with minimal equipment.
  • University of Minnesota Extension: Small-scale hydroponics Beginner guide for growing plants without soil using water, nutrients, and light.
  • Clemson HGIC: Container vegetable gardening Grow vegetables in pots or buckets when you do not have good soil or space.
  • Seed Library Map Find seed libraries (often at public libraries) that offer free seeds.
  • National Center for Home Food Preservation Safe ways to store and preserve food.
  • US Forest Service: Wildflower ethics General ethics for protecting native plants and habitats.
  • US Forest Service: Collection permits When permits may be required to collect plants or plant material on national forests.
  • FarmHack Open community for DIY farm tools, repairs, and low-cost inventions for food growing.
  • WikiHouse Modular building system with open designs and guides for lower-cost building projects.
  • Open Source Ecology Open-source blueprints for practical machines and tools to support self-reliance.

AI learning and help (free access options)

  • ChatGPT (free sign up option) Ask for help with plans, resumes, forms, step-by-step checklists, and learning topics.
  • Grok (xAI) AI assistant for questions, planning, and research-style help.
  • Google Gemini AI assistant for writing, planning, brainstorming, and learning.
  • Microsoft Copilot AI assistant for questions, drafting, and learning support.
  • Perplexity AI answer engine that can help find sources and summarize information.

Fishing resources (nationwide)

  • US Fish and Wildlife Service: Purchase a fishing license Explains fishing licenses and links to buy licenses (state-based).
  • Take Me Fishing: Get your fishing license State lookup for license basics, where to buy, and rules.
  • Take Me Fishing: Places to boat and fish State lookup for fishing locations and regulations.
  • NOAA Fisheries: Federal fishing permits by region Federal permit info (mainly for regulated fisheries and reporting by region).

Veterans

  • VA: Homeless Veterans Housing, health care, and support services for veterans.
  • VA National Call Center for Homeless Veterans Call 877-4AID-VET (877-424-3838) for help.

Legal help and documents

  • Legal Services Corporation: Find legal aid Find free or low-cost civil legal help (housing, benefits, family, more).
  • SSA: Replace a Social Security card How to replace or request a Social Security card.
  • CDC: Where to write for vital records How to request birth certificates and other vital records (state-based).
  • USA.gov: State motor vehicle services Find your state DMV site for ID or drivers license info.

Libraries, internet, phone access

  • Find a public library Libraries often provide internet, printing, computers, and help with job searches.
  • Lifeline Support Discount phone or internet service for eligible households.

Start a small business (SBA and free help)

  • SBA Learning Platform Free online courses to start, pivot, or grow a small business.
  • SBA: Find local assistance Find free counseling and training partners by ZIP code (SBDC, SCORE, and others).
  • SCORE Free business mentoring and workshops (partnered with SBA).

Public lands and outdoor info

  • US Forest Service (home) Official site for national forests and grasslands, rules, recreation info, and permits.
